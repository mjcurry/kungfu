# Style guide

This guide captures the writing conventions used by the document skill.
Keep it short — entries should describe a rule and how to apply it.

## Tone

Write in plain, declarative sentences. Prefer the active voice. Replace
qualifiers ("rather", "somewhat", "fairly") with specifics or remove
them outright. The reader's time is the most expensive thing in the room.

## Structure

Open with a single sentence that states the document's purpose. Use
headings for navigation, not for emphasis. Prefer two short sections
over one long one. End with a clear next step when the document is
asking the reader to do something.

## Formatting

- Use sentence case for headings.
- Use Oxford commas.
- Use Markdown links rather than bare URLs.
- Avoid emoji unless it is conveying information that prose cannot.
- Use ISO 8601 dates unless the document has a known publication date
  that makes relative dates unambiguous.

## What to avoid

- Placeholder words that stand in for thinking — replace them with the
  actual items.
- "Should" without an actor: say who should do the thing.
- Footnotes for content the reader needs; put it inline.
